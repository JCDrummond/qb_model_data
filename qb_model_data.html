Our Question

Passer rating is a metric used to evaluate quarterbacks in the NFL, and it is calculated based on a quarterbacks completions, yards gained, touchdowns, interceptions and attempts.

How does passer rating relate to success? We can use the number of wins a team has when a particular quarterback tarts as a meausre of success. And then the questons we are trying to answer now are:

1. Do quarterbacks with a higher passer rating have more wins?

2. Do quarterbacks with a lower passer rating have fewer wins?

3. How many wins can we expect to get when we have a tarting quarterback with a particular passer rating?

Simple Linear Regression

qbinf <- lm(win_percentage ~ passer_rating, data = filter_df)
tidy(qbinf, conf.int = TRUE)

## 2 passer_rating     1.12     0.234      4.81 0.0000551    0.644      1.61

The Intercept linear regression values, represent the win percentage and witht the estimate percentage value when the QB passer rating is 0 is -50.7. If we put this in real world terms, that team that had a QB passer rating of 0 wouldnt ever win a game, it would be theoretically impossible from these estimations. With the high and low confidence ratings also in the negatives, it confirms to us that a passer rating of 0 is a loss, no matter how many games you played.

The passer rating values, however, confirm our initial thoughts of the linear relationship, as the slope with an estimate of 1.12, demostrates that if a players passing rating increases by 1, the winning percentage will increase by 1.12, with a low confidence level of a 0.644 rise and a high confidence level of a 1.61 rise. This shows that the their is correlation but it is a very minor correlation between these two variables.

Independence of Observations

Here well determine if our linear regression meets the assumption of independence of observations through the Durbin Watson Test.

car::durbinWatsonTest(qbinf)

We observe that our D-W Statistic of 2.372391 is close to the recommeded value of 2. The satisfies the assumptions of indenpence of observations so there isnt a need to consder another type of analysis, such as a time series analysis or linear mixed models for now.

Interpretation

Passing Rating alone, although it showed a linear relationship with the Winning Percentage, it was a weak relationship, which indicates that there are many more underlying factors at play rather than the passer rating. Yes, in NFL, the QB is the most important player, and with a poor QB, teams usually struggle to compete as they dictate the pace and style of the offence. The reason why it is a weak linear relationship though, is that there are multiple factors that can influence a game. For example, on offence, a lot of teams play a rushing yard style play, that doesnt affect the QBs passer rating positively or negatively. On top of that, if the defence is poor in their RBs and LBs the opposition QBs passing game will be significantly strengthened as the risk of throwing pick 6s are reduced as they have time to sit in the pocket and pick their ideal route. If a teams defence gives up a lot of points, the QB passer rating wont be the main influence of the winning percentage.
